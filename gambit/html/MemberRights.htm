Legal Opinion: Members' Rights to Audits, Financial Statements and Accounting Records

By Wilder Wadford, NCCA Attorney
July 24, 2009

My opinion is based upon a review of statutory law on the above issue.

A member on a non-profit corporation, after giving the Board written notice of at least 5 days, is entitled to inspect and copy, at a reasonable time and location specified by the Board, the financial statements, if any, that have been furnished or would have been required to be furnished to a member under the By-Laws during the past three years.

Under the NCCA By-Laws, the Board is required to issue an annual statement only. According to North Carolina law, the Board of a non-profit corporation does not have to give any other financial statements, accounting records or to an audit.

Modern non-profit corporate law favors disclosure, but also favors the power in the Board to deny claims for records by members who might use the this as a means to make burdensome or frivolous requests to the Board.

I have been a two-term President of the Association and have decades of experience dealing with small corporations. Members of most non-profit organizations generally do not do the work of the corporation, the Board and a small group of supporters do. The Board must be free to do its work without needless interference by members. Board members are, of course, ultimately responsible to their membership. If they are not reasonably responsive to the membership then they cannot effectively achieve the goals of the membership. It is a balance which must be maintained but there can only be one hand on the wheel in the Association and that is the Board.

Anyone who has been on the NCCA Board and effectively held their positions and met the needs of the membership in general knows that the power must be concentrated in the Board. The Association has a modern set of By-Laws and Articles of Incorporation suited to its organization and purposes which give that necessary power to the Board to effectively do its work and, in good hands, meet the needs of the general membership.
