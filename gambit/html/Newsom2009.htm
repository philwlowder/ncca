A Message from Gary Newsom
NCCA President

Sept. 28 -- As you all know, Victor Beaman has resigned as NCCA President and I have been elected by the board to serve the remainder of his term, which will end at the business meeting that will be held at the 2010 NC Open.  

I have become fond of Victor during the time I have known him. He is a good man. And as he leaves we should remember his successes. During his short tenure as President, the organization showed a positive cash flow and now has more adult members than it has had in several years. Kudos to Victor and I hope that moving forward we can build on these successes. I know I speak for everyone when I say thanks, Victor, and good luck in your future endeavors. I'm sure we will see you at the chess board again soon! 

I want to let you all know that I don't plan on just marking time during the next year plus. It seems to me that we are in a good spot right now to move the organization forward. To recall history once again...there were some lean years in the recent past when the organization was basically out of money. Neal Harris stepped in and righted the ship, followed by Randy Wheeless, who not only kept things going, but contributed positively to our current financial standing, which as mentioned earlier is pretty good. They had the tough job ... getting the NCCA on a sound financial footing again. Now in my mind, it's time to address some more basic concerns that have come back to the front burner after the successes of our leaders of the recent past.  

Here is an outline of my sense of what needs to happen and what I plan to do about it. I would love to hear feedback from everyone to let me know if I am on the right path or if I have overlooked something important: 

1) Events "owned" by the NCCA - Our organization "owns" some events. These events are the NC Open, the NC Class, the NC Scholastics Championship, and the NC Invitational. There may be another event or two that should be on this list. Possibly the Girls tournament to qualify a state rep for the national girls' event. Moving forward, these tournaments should be controlled by the NCCA. I think we've done a good job with the scholastics. It's a money maker and I think Neal realized that and made it the centerpiece of his strategy to get us back in the black. The Invitational, though not a money maker for the organization, is also in good shape.

Moving forward into 2010, we are going to control the NC Class and NC Open. That means that there will be a bidding system set up (Charlie Carerros is already working on a proposed bidding system...thanks for the good work Charlie!) and we will accept bids from whomever wants to run the events. In general, a successful bid will be one that provides a good location and a fair EF to prize ratio. We of course also want the organizer to be successful with the event. If no acceptable bid comes in, then it will be incumbent upon the NCCA to put on the tournament. While on the subject of events, the 2010 Scholastics has been taken on by Bill DeArmey and will be held at the Koury Convention Center in Greensboro. We are working with Bill Clausen in Raleigh on the 2011 event. Not official yet, but we are working on the details. 

2) Website improvements - The website will improve over the next year. We will have a games database and more pictorial reports from events and clubs. I subscribe to the "slow and steady wins the race" philosophy. Don't look for one big splash followed by burnout, but rather constant, sustainable improvements as time goes on. 

3) Continued Fiscal Responsibility - We are in a good financial place and we will continue along that path. We will be conservative with the budget but not paralyzed with fear of doing something wrong! While building up the Queen City Chess Club I have taken several small leaps of faith with prize funds or other incentives that ended up increasing our numbers and/or the excitement level at our events. I am not opposed to the idea of taking small leaps of faith in this role if it seems appropriate. At this point in our non-profit organization, it seems to me that the success of the leadership should be judged in how effectively we grow the number of members and how happy the members are with the events and the other benefits of being a member. As long as we have a comfortable nest egg that is not being used inappropriately, I don't really see that taking our net worth from x to y should be a significant factor.  

Now here are some general observations and opinions about random subjects that affect NC Chess, to let you know what I'm thinking. Please chime in with opinions on the message board.

* The NC Open should be held in the Triad area on a regular basis. It's halfway between Charlotte and Raleigh. Also an easy drive from Asheville. I am not in favor of this event rotating. Adult chess players can drive to a centrally located event.

* The State Scholastics, on the other hand would serve us well to rotate between Charlotte/ The Triad/ and Raleigh, and maybe Asheville. That gives each city a chance to have a feature event to build scholastics chess around.

* Our state champion should be the winner of the Invitational, not the top finisher in the NC Open. I am in favor of the NC Open being an open event, to maximize the number of players. But it makes for a potentially random state champ, in which someone who gets a lucky last round pairing could win it all. Our state champ should be the player who proves himself against the toughest competition. And that is at the Invitational. We should still use the Open to qualify two people for the Invitational, as we have in the past. Of course this is a suggestion that would have to be voted on. I include it here just as a thought starter. Anyone have an opinion on this idea?

* I would like to ask that the message board be used as a sounding board for the improvement of the organization. Now I know that things aren't always perfect and people have legitimate gripes and concerns at times. I just ask that these be brought up in a positive manner in the spirit of getting things right. We have gone through a period where Victor (and sometimes I) has been beaten up pretty badly. And in my opinion, usually unnecessarily.

Let's enter into a new era where we respect each other for what we have accomplished or are trying to accomplish. And let's give each other the benefit of reasonable doubt when issues arise. I sincerely hope that the message board becomes very busy. And people are every day using it to exchange pertinent information and help us move the organization forward. Please don't hesitate to let me know how you feel...right, wrong, or indifferent. I hope to see more people who have stayed on the sidelines get involved on the message board.

* I am open to suggestions about continuing the North Carolina Grand Prix. In my opinion, it has been of only marginal interest (partly due to the fact that we didn't push it very hard after the first couple of months). I think at the very least, the formula should be simplified. Please let us know your opinions on that. 

Well that does it for now. I am honored to have the opportunity to serve the organization as President. I will do my best to maintain and hopefully build on the successes of the recent past.

Thank you

Gary Newsom, NCCA President
