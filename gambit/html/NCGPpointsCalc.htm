How Points Are Calculated

 

 

It is not too complicated if you would like to check your own points.  If you win, first determine your section.  Not your place within that section, but the section level. 

Second, figure the prize level of the section.  Then find your base points on the chart below. 

Next, see if your section qualifies for bonus points.  Twelve or more entries in a section doubles your points, and certain tournaments qualify for triple points.

The last step is to figure your points based on your place finish in the section.  That is at the bottom, and is a percentage.

Let's do an example.  If you are playing in the second section of a NC Grand Prix event, let's say you won 2nd place outright.  The total prizes in the section were $250.00.  This makes the base points for the section 80.  For the example, we will say there were 12 people in the section.  That doubles the points to 160 points.  Last step:  30% of 160 points gives you 48 points.  You have earned 48 points! 

Ties for points will be awarded as per USCF rules regarding cash prizes.  If two people tie for 1st place, they will divide the sum of both 1st and 2nd place points.

Now for a slightly tricky twist (which was added to ensure that higher sections always pay more than lower sections):  If the next lowest section has more people, and if this would affect bonus points, then the average of the two sections will be used.  For example, if the TOP SECTION has 10 entries, and the 2nd SECTION has 12, then the average is 11.  Hang on, what if the 3rd section has 14 players?  Bingo!  The three together would equal 36 players, divided by three gives us 12.  That means all three sections would get bonus points.  This averaging would continue until either the top section got a bonus, or no section got a bonus.  It's a little complicated, but we think it is fair.
