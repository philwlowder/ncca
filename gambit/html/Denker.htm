N.C. State Scholastic -- Denker Criteria

Here is the criteria the N.C. Chess Association will use to decide who will be the state's representative at the Denker Tournament of High School Champions in August in Indianapolis - held in conjunction with the U.S. Open.

The state rep will come from the K-12 Open Section. However, the Denker rep must be a player who is in the 9th grade or above. That is a Denker rule - not ours

1)    If there is a sole winner, that person will win be the Denker rep. Alternatives will follow via tiebreak order.

2)    If there is a tie between two players – those players will have a match on another day to settle the Denker rep.

3)    If three or more players tie for first, the Denker rep will be settled by the tiebreakers used by the tournament. However, if one of the three is not a high school student, a playoff between the two high schoolers will be used to determine the winner.

4)   If the winner of the K-12 Open tournament is not a high school student, the first high school player via tiebreaks will be the Denker rep. However, the scholarship will go the the top finishers -- regardless of grade.
