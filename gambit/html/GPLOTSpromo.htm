North Carolina Grand Prix

Rules Clarification to Help NC Players at LOTS

 

A recent clarification of the NC Grand Prix rules makes the Land of the Sky tournament a can't miss opportunity for NC players competing in the series.  The rules had been unclear as to how out of state players might affect the competition.  The short answer is:  not at all!

Here is the text of the recent amendment:

Additional Rules and Guidelines:

D.  Players from other states are not be eligible to compete for NCGP points, but their points will be rolled over to the next eligible North Carolina player.  If a NC player ties with an out of state player, the NC player will receive full points for their place.  In other words, out of state players will not be factored into any point calculations, place finishes, or splits. 

This means there are LOTS of NC Grand Prix points up for grabs!  Players skip this tournament at their own risk, and may find themselves licking their wounds later as they scramble to catch those savvy competitors who made LOTS a part of their chess calendar.

Folks, I write this from a cozy cottage in Weaverville, my temporary headquarters and LOTS launching pad.  I guarantee you it is worth the trip to Asheville for the great views alone!  Land of the Sky gets my vote for "best dressed" tournament.  Couple this with a reasonable entry fee and great guaranteed prizes, and this is a no-brainer.  (Seriously, save your brainpower for the tournament; you will need it!)

Be there, or be square.  You won't be board.  Hah!
