The 2009 North Carolina Grand Prix

 Official Procedures, Rules, and Regulations

 

The North Carolina Grand Prix (NCGP) is a seasonal competition somewhat similar to the USCF Grand Prix.  Points are awarded to section winners at participating tournaments on a sliding scale (detailed below*), with slightly more points awarded to winners in higher sections. 

Our inaugural season is shorter, and runs from January 1, 2009 through the 2009 NC Open.  For 2010, the season will start immediately after the 2009 NC Open and run through the 2010 NC Open.  Each year, the NC Open will be your final chance to earn valuable points.  This will add to the excitement of our annual championship event.   

The North Carolina Grand Prix was designed to encourage NCCA membership, and to promote tournament participation.  This exciting series is open to any NC resident who is a current paid NCCA member.  Scholastic players may also participate by paying regular adult dues. 

To qualify as a NC resident, one must reside in North Carolina for at least six months of the year.  Military and college students who meet this requirement will be considered eligible.  Participants who relocate will not forfeit previously earned points if this six month requirement is met.

*Earning NCGP Points:  Points are earned at participating tournaments by finishing 1st, 2nd, or 3rd in any section.  If no third place is awarded in a section, then the third place finish will be considered third place.  All winners will receive a one point bonus for each NCGP event they compete in during the season.       

Awarded points will be calculated based on the following:

Section Level:  Top Section winners receive the highest percentage of points, 20% higher than the next lower section.  Subsequent lower sections drop at 20% for each level.

Section Size:  Sections with <12 players get 1x points, Sections with 12 or more players get 2x points, and tournaments of 100 players or more will receive an extra 1x points for each section (up to 3x points, unless the average section is <12, then 2x points).

Section Payout:  the higher the total prize fund per section, the more points one may potentially earn.

In a nutshell, this means the bigger the tournament, the more NCGP points one might earn. 

Prizes:  Prizes are not guaranteed, but conservative estimates indicate a minimum total prize fund of $600.00 for the season.  We will offer regular updates on this projection.  The more you play, the more we pay!

The prize fund is generated by a $1.00 fee per adult player (and per participating scholastic player) at NCGP tournaments.  Tournament organizers at participating tournaments are responsible for these fees, which are to be remitted within seven business days to the NCCA treasurer to keep in a special fund.  

 

Final Standings:  Cash prizes will be awarded in the following manner:

1st:  50% of the total prize fund

2nd:  30% of the total prize fund

<1800:  15% of the total prize fund

<1600:  5% of the total prize fund

<1400:  East Carolina Chess will match the <1600 prize fund

Eligibility for the <1800, <1600, and <1400 prizes will be determined by the average USCF published rating for the season.

Additional Rules and Guidelines:

A.  Chief Tournament Directors and/or Organizers may not earn points in their own event.  Any such points will be rolled over to the next eligible player.  For example, if a tournament director won 1st place, the 2nd place finisher would receive those points, the 3rd place finisher would receive 2nd, and so forth.  In other words, such players will not be factored into point calculations, place finishes, or splits.

B.  The NCCA President, Vice Presidents, and Treasurer are not eligible for any points or prizes in series.  Any designee responsible for tabulating results will also be ineligible to earn points.  Any such points will be rolled over as described for Chief Tournament Directors (A).

C.  If an ineligible NC scholastic player wins points, the points will not roll over to another player.  Scholastic players may redeem their points as described below (J).

D.  Players from other states are not be eligible to compete for NCGP points, but their points will be rolled over to the next eligible North Carolina player.  If a NC player ties with an out of state player, the NC player will receive full points for their place.  In other words, out of state players will not be factored into any point calculations, place finishes, or splits. 

E.  Weekday tournaments are ineligible to participate in the series.

F.  Each USCF affiliate is allowed one NCGP tournament per month.

G.  Tournaments must be Open to all USCF members regardless of rating or other factors.  Accordingly, no scholastic tournaments or sections are eligible for this series.  Sections may only be restricted by rating.

H.  If a lower section has more entries than a higher section, then both sections will be combined and divided equally for purposes of computing Section Size.

I.  Participating tournaments must require NCCA Membership for all NC adult players.  Any player nineteen years or older will be considered an adult.  If an adult player inadvertently finishes a tournament without paying NCCA dues, this will be deducted from any tournament prize. 

J.  Scholastic players have a choice of whether to participate.  Scholastic players who have not remitted NCCA membership dues may do so within 30 days of a win to keep any potential points they would have earned.  Thereafter, such points shall expire.  

K.  The NCCA President will be responsible for determining and certifying the final standings.  The points tally may be maintained by the NCCA President or a designee. 

L.  Prizes will be awarded within no more than 30 days of the end of each season.    

M.  Points are provisional until funding is received by the NCCA, and the USCF rating report is up.   

N.  Any player caught cheating at a NCGP event will forfeit no less than half of their NCGP points for the year.  This will be at the sole discretion of the NCCA.

O.  The NCCA reserves the right to make minor alterations to the procedures, rules, and regulations.  The NCCA reserves the right to settle any dispute arising as a result of this series, and their decision will be final.
