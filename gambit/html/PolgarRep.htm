Being the Polgar Rep from North Carolina

Here is the criteria the N.C. Chess Association will use to decide who will be the state's representative to the GM Susan Polgar National Invitational for Girls, which will be held this summer at Texas Tech University in Lubbock, Texas.

The state's rep will come from the Championship Section of the All Girls Open -- April 26 in Matthews. The state rep must have been a scholastic player during that year and be under the age 19 by Aug. 15, 2008. The N.C. Chess Association will pay expenses up to $950 to attend the event.

1)    If there is a sole winner of the All Girls Open, that girl will be the Polgar rep. Alternates will follow via tiebreak order.

2)    If there is a tie between two players – those players will have a match on another day to settle the Polgar rep. The match will most likely occur alongside a future existing tournament -- following that tournament's time control. It will be a two-game match - with a third game if necessary. If a tie still exists, a G/10 will follow until a winner in determined. 

3)    If three or more players tie for first, the Polgar rep will be settled by the tiebreakers used by the All Girls Open.
