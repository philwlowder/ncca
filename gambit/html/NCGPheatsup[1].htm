North Carolina Grand Prix Series Heats Up

 

 

The first two tournaments in the NC Grand Prix series have been huge successes.  Combined, The Asheboro Open 41 and Reverse Angle 12 drew a whopping 100 players!  Over $1800 in cash prizes were awarded at these events, but that’s just the beginning.  Thirty new NCCA members have signed up as excitement builds regarding the NCGP series.

The competition for 2nd place is a virtual free-for-all among a diverse group!  I love it!!  FM Chumachenko has a good lead for now, but this could evaporate quickly if he skips some tournaments, or if he doesn’t play in the LOTS, LPO, or NCO.  The points formula Victor created may seem complicated to some, but I maintain that is ingeniously fair and gives everyone a chance.  Even a casual glance at the current standings supports this hypothesis.  

 

NCCA membership is required at all participating tournaments for adults, but many savvy scholastic players are choosing to participate by paying regular adult dues of just $5.00 per year.  In tough economic times like these, this is a real bargain that goes a long way to support North Carolina chess. 

 

NCCA members are competing for the following prizes in this series:  1st, 2nd, Under 1800, and Under 1600.  The “Under” prizes will be based on one’s average published USCF rating during the series.  All prizes will be awarded as per USCF tournament guidelines.  We don’t have an exact dollar figure, but conservative estimates project a minimum of a $600.00 total prize fund.  Personally, I believe we can hit $1000.00 or more! 

 

The more you play, the more we pay!

 

There are two more NCGP events scheduled for this month.  January 17 will be the first TACO of the year in Raleigh.  If you have a BIG appetite for chess, you can’t go wrong at the TACO, with four rounds of Game/70 action!  Regular competitors include NC Champion FM Ron Simpson, who may be ready to light up the score board with some NCGP points of his own!

 

Folks, you absolutely don’t want to miss North Carolina’s largest chess tournament on January 23-25th.  The entry fee is remarkably low considering the GUARANTEED PRIZES, and Wilder Wadford always puts on a great event.  Heck, even the hotel rates are great!  What do you have to lose?  Make some chess memories that will last a lifetime.  Oh, did I mention that you can earn TRIPLE NC Grand Prix points?  Yes You Can! 

 

Is it just me, or is NC Chess on a roll this year?
