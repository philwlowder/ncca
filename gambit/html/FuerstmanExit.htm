A Farewell to NM Leland Fuerstman

by Wilder Wadford, NCCA Attorney
Aug. 14, 2009

In an unexpected turn of events, NM Leland Fuerstman has renounced his membership in the North Carolina Chess Association (NCCA). I had assumed, based upon verbal and written assurances from NM Fuerstman that all issues regarding the 2009 Scholastic Tournament had been resolved, including NM Fuerstman posting on his website, for a period of 30 days, the results of the agreement as summarized in my article which is currently on the NCCA home board (“Resolution”) and is scheduled to be  removed after August 23, 2009.

NM Fuerstman agreed to the posting of the Resolution verbally in a telephone conversation with me and, in fact, did activate an NCCA link on his website for a few days. NM Fuerstman abruptly decided on August 6, 2009, to renege on his agreement to post the Resolution on his site despite having agreed to do so and after having an opportunity to read the Resolution before its publishing. In addition, he has reversed his agreement concerning member’s legal rights to the financial records, the financial findings of the Board and presumably his apology.

The Board is obviously displeased with NM Fuerstman’s renunciation, which is his acknowledgment of the non existence of the NCCA. He has requested that any and all references to him including being in the Hall of Fame be removed from the NCCA website and that his disassociation is total. The terms of the agreed upon Resolution cannot be carried out due to his voluntary removal as a participant in that Resolution. However, the Board must nevertheless conclude this matter in NM Fuerstman’s voluntary absence.

On August 13, 2009, NM Fuerstman voluntarily removed his renunciation from his website.  This is a good move and shows his intention to bring this matter to a close. The Board agrees; however, his renunciation, nevertheless, cannot go unanswered, not necessarily point by point, but in general because the Board does not want to perpetuate endless discussion of this episode but does desire to draw a line under it and have an opportunity for general rebuttal.

NM Fuerstman’s renunciation is regrettable. His contributions to North Carolina chess are well known and his departure from the NCCA community is a loss. He and I have been friends for 30 years and he now refers to me in the past tense in this context. That, of course, is a personal loss to me which I take no pleasure in. However, my pole star is to be a guardian of what is best for the NCCA and the Resolution was what I had hoped for, a reconciliation. The Board leaves open the opportunity for NM Fuerstman, even now, to reapply for membership in the NCCA, subject to the Board’s approval. For now, the Board, in view of the renunciation, considers NM Fuerstman’s membership privileges and other entitlements indefinitely suspended. I personally hope that the application is made. I also personally add that I have no malicious or biased intentions against NM Fuerstman. We simply disagree on the law and the facts, which happens every day in my profession, but I do not take it personally.

The Board can validly contest every relevant and material issue raised in NM Fuerstman’s renunciation, but we are tired of continuous refutations and revisitations of issues completely resolved in the vast majority of interested minds. Eventually there is an “end on it”.  There is much more that can be said in solid defense of the Board’s position, but, ultimately, the Board believes nothing more should be said in the interest of peace and quiet - other than the essence of the episode - NM Fuerstman agreed to everything in the Resolution and now he does not. Eventually one must just make a judgment and just stop arguing. 

We hold no grudges either NM Fuerstman. I hope you will reconsider. Neither I nor the Board want to silence civil free speech and responsible inquiry. However, there are more responsible and respectful ways of doing this rather than a trial by “flash” publicity. Surely, there are more important issues to be discussed on the chess websites - like new variations, a particularly beautiful game and reasonable discussions of opinion about all matters affecting chess. I look forward to that civil discourse. Meanwhile, here is my Farewell to Fuerstman, which I hope will be temporary.
