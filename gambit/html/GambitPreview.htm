Chess Autobiography

 

by Charles ‘Kit’ Crittenden

 

 

"Tom Hales has asked me to write something about my chess adventures.  He has also sent a set of questions to be addressed; some of these go beyond the boundaries of the chess world itself, and so my comments are not limited to tournament play.  My focus will be on what is particularly interesting to NC players."

 

I hate to tease you like this folks, but the full six-page article will be published in the next

NC Gambit!  A fascinating read!

 

Kit Crittenden was the youngest state champion ever at 14 years old, a record that still stands

after more than sixty years!  He went on to win the North Carolina Championship a total of

five times, and was the first Chess Master in North Carolina history.  (See clip from 1960 Gambit

featured below)
