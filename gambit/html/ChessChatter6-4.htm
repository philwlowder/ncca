North Carolina Chess Chatter
By Gary Newsom, NCCA Vice President
 

  • Denker/Polgar Reps
  • Congratulations to Chris Mabe
  • NC Class in September
  • Notable Results at the LPO and Asheboro

The purpose of this column is to inform and enlighten chess players from around North Carolina about chess events, personalities, and results from around the state. If you have any information for us to use or questions you would like answered, please feel free to send an e-mail to gary.newsom@queencitychess.com. Let’s use this space to celebrate and enjoy all the good things that happen every day in the NC Chess world. Thanks for reading!

 

Denker and Polgar reps.  Congratulations once again and thank you to the fine young folks who represented North Carolina in the recent national high school events, the Denker for state champions and the Polgar for the top girls. Indira Puri, Nicole Spencer and David High all did their state proud with their play in the events. We hope to see them back in regular statewide competition again soon!

 

Congratulations to Chris Mabe. Not only did he win clear first at the LPO, her raised his rating above 2300 for the first time! I see Chris quite a bit and I can vouch for the fact that he is playing very powerful chess right now. And of course, results don’t lie. I know Chris will be looking to maintain this level and then make an assault on 2400 and International Titles. Best of luck in all such endeavors Chris!

 

NC Class in September.  One of the state’s traditional tournaments is making a comeback this year after a long layoff. The NC Class Championships will be held in Charlotte in September. Our good friend from Georgia, Thad Rogers, is putting on the event at a very nice venue, the University Hilton in Charlotte. This is a good location, not far from Charlotte Motor Speedway and the Concord Mills Mall if you or those you are coming with are looking for a bit of sightseeing or shopping. Abundant restaurant choices are nearby, a good many in walking distance. Please make every effort to support this event, which should be the “fourth major” on the NC tournament schedule.

 

Notable results at the LPO and Asheboro.  In addition to the aforementioned result by Chris Mabe, I want to mention a few other noteworthy performances. David Erb scored 2 wins and 2 draws (he took a rd 4 bye) against 3 masters (One an IM) and an expert (yours truly) and raised his rating above 2000 for the first time in many years. Todd Trower had a strong second place finish in the U2200 section. Yuval Shemesh won the U2000 and raised his rating to 2000. Michael Lipkin made a nice run and finished tied for second in that section. A heartwarming result in the tournament started by his father. Congrats to Michael for that achievement. Cheng Chu continued “chewing” (sorry, couldn’t resist) up the competition by going 5-0 in the U1800. Matt Embler won the U1600 and long time Charlotte player Spencer Singleton came in a strong second. Winston-Salem's Leo Poppante and Charlotte's Josh Holcombe came in first and second respectively in the U1400. And at the most recent Asheboro Open, Statesville wunderkind William Campbell cracked the 1900 rating mark for the first time. Watch out for this guy. He is only 11, I think.

 

Thanks for reading. I’ll update again soon!  Gary Newsom...NCCA Vice President
