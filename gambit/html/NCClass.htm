FM Ron Simpson/ NM Aliyev Come Out on Top N.C. Class

Raleigh chess stalwart Ron Simpson and South Carolina's Timur Aliyev tied for first in the North Carolina Class Championship, held at the comfortable University Hilton in Charlotte on September 11-13.. The two played to a final round draw to share the prize. Simpson also allowed a second round draw to Virginia's Rusty Potter while Aliyev drew in the same round with Maurice Dana. The other noteworthy result turned in by a NC player in this section was a strong 3-2 showing by Independence High School's Dominique Myers, who defeated Dana in round 4 and had a chance to catch the leaders in round 5, where he was defeated by Georgia's Damir Studen.

On to the class sections, where invitations to next year's Border Battle were on the line. The expert section was won by Eric Patchell of Kentucky with 4 1/2.  Yuval Shemesh, who has stepped up his game and is playing very strong chess right now, was the top NC finisher with 3, and so qualifies for the 2010 Border Battle. The second Border Battle spot was won by Ken Baxter with 2.5 Though not a current expert, he has held the rating in the past and deserves the spot based on his result. Here's to hoping that this inspires Ken to get his Expert's rating back! First alternate is Dan Liu,

The A section resulted in a tie between Charlotte's Alain Morais and South Carolina's John Vonderleith. Alain is playing very strong chess right now and went undefeated with two draws. Joining him as a Border Battle qualifier is Patrick McCartney, who edged out Cheng Chu by 1/2 point on the fourth tiebreaker. Next tiebreak would have been head to head, which would have been won by Chu, who will, of course, be the first alternate.

In the B section, after the dust settled, there was a three way tie among NC players with John Brooks, Brian Moore and Derek Zhang all scoring 4-1. After tiebreaks, the qualifiers for Border Battle were Moore and Zhang. Brooks will be the first alternate.

The C section was taken Suryabrata Dutta of Georgia with 4.5. Top NC finishers were Charlie Carerreos, Ian Haigler and Joseph Micklos. Qualifying for the Border Battle on tiebreaks were Haigler and Micklos. Carerros will be the first alternate.

The D section was taken by Bruce Clyne of Georgia and NC high school student Leo Poppante. Joining him as a Border Battle qualifier is Drew Holloway, another high school student from Charlotte. The E section qualifiers for Border Battle were Paul Ingram and Jay M Robinson middle school student Veronica Skoczek. Ron Futerman is first alternate.

The comeback of Charlotte area adult chess continues as the event drew 109 players from 6 different states, making this the largest adult event in the city in many years. Thanks to all who participated. We'll try to make this event even bigger and better next year.

Gary Newsom, NCCA Vice President
