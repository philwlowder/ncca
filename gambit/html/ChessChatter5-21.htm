North Carolina Chess Chatter
                               By Gary Newsom, NCCA Vice President, Sept. 7, 2009
 

  • NC Class Championships September 11-13
  • Laurin Keller Memorial Chess Club
  • Scholastics Chess gets back in gear

The purpose of this column is to inform and enlighten chess players from around North Carolina about chess events, personalities, and results from around the state. If you have any information for us to use or questions you would like answered, please feel free to send an e-mail to gary.newsom@queencitychess.com. Let’s use this space to celebrate and enjoy all the good things that happen every day in the NC Chess world. Thanks for reading!  

  • 2010 Border Battle slots up for grabs at the NC Class.  The Border Battle match up between The Great North State and our friendly competitors down south in The Palmetto State is becoming a popular and much anticipated event on the chess calendar. With that in mind, I’m pleased to announce that official criteria for qualifying to represent NC in this event has been decided upon. Starting next weekend with the NC Class, the top two NC residents in the U2200, U2000, U1800, U1600, and U1400 sections will be the automatic qualifiers for Border Battle. The top two NC residents rated 1199 or below from the U1400 section will also qualify. The feature matches….those between our state champions and state high school champions, will still be determined the same way as previously; but all “class” section qualifiers will be determined next weekend. Oh, and while I’m on the subject of the NC Class…don’t forget that it is a NC Grand Prix event and a good turnout will help raise that prize fund considerably. (I think the kitty is currently in the $400’s). Also, please remember that there are class winners in the NCGP as well as the grand prize winner. Victor is busy updating the class standings as this is being written. Look for an update on those totals very soon.

  • The Laurin Keller Memorial Chess Club.  I was fortunate to have the opportunity to visit this club last Thursday night. The venue was familiar to me, as I lived in the Triad in the late ‘80s and early ‘90s. The Greensboro Chess Club met in that very same location way back then. Back in those days, a visit to the Folk Recreation Center usually meant getting my clock cleaned in speed chess against NC Hall of Fame member David Steele. No sign of David as I walked in the door last week, but there was plenty of good competition with the likes of club founder and all around good guy Josh Lawson, Cheng Chu, Zakir Ahmed, Joe Graves and others in attendance. We played a closely contested speed chess tourney. It was a good experience. I will be back and heartily recommend a Thursday night visit for those in driving distance of Greensboro. By the way, since the club is in memory of Laurin Keller…my own memories of him. Laurin always impressed me as being a gregarious guy with a good smile and a welcoming personality. And he defeated me (He was black in a Benko Gambit) in Asheboro a couple of years ago in our only tournament game.
